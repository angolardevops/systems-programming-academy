This Academy exists to teach **software engineering**, not just programming
languages. Syntax is the easy part and it is well covered elsewhere. What is
rarely taught well — and what separates a coder from an engineer — is how to
**design systems, reason about trade-offs, test rigorously, and ship software
that survives production**.

We do that through three languages, chosen because their design philosophies
disagree in instructive ways:

- **Python** optimises for the programmer's time and clarity.
- **Go** optimises for teams and operational simplicity.
- **Rust** optimises for correctness and control without a garbage collector.

Seeing the *same* problem solved in all three teaches you what each design
choice actually costs.

## Who this is for

- Beginners who want to learn to program **the way it is done in industry** from day one.
- Working engineers who know one language and want to think across paradigms.
- Universities and companies looking for a rigorous, freely reusable curriculum.

<Aside type="tip" title="You do not need to know all three languages">
  Each lesson is self-contained and states its prerequisites. You can follow a
  single language track top-to-bottom, or read comparatively.
</Aside>

## The standards every lesson is held to

1. **Everything executes.** No pseudo-code. Companion code lives under
   [`examples/`](https://github.com/angolardevops) and is compiled and tested in CI.
2. **Everything is tested.** Each lesson ships with runnable tests; we show you the
   command and the expected output.
3. **Benchmarks are real.** Performance claims come with a binary you can run.
4. **Mistakes are first-class.** Every lesson has a *Common mistakes* section showing
   the actual compiler/runtime error and the fix.
5. **Interview-ready.** Each lesson ends with interview questions and a challenge.

## How to read a lesson

Every Foundations lesson follows the same rhythm:

- **Theory** — the mental model, with a diagram.
- **Examples** — small, runnable, explained line by line.
- **Common mistakes & debugging** — real errors and how to read them.
- **Exercises** — with complete solutions.
- **Performance** — measured, not asserted.
- **Best practices vs anti-patterns** — side by side.
- **Interview questions & challenge** — to test real understanding.
